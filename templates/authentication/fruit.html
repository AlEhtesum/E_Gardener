Grow Fresh Fruits

Mango

Mango trees produce delicious, juicy fruits that are rich in vitamins A and C. The trees have lush, evergreen leaves and can grow quite tall, providing ample shade. They thrive in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Orange

Orange trees are famous for their sweet and tangy fruits, which are a great source of vitamin C. The trees have glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. They prefer warm climates, full sun, and well-drained soil.

Papaya

Papaya plants bear large, sweet fruits that are high in vitamins A and C. They have a tropical appearance with large, lobed leaves and a single central trunk. Papayas thrive in warm, sunny locations with well-drained soil.

Cherry

Cherry trees produce sweet or tart fruits that are enjoyed fresh or used in various culinary dishes. They have attractive spring blossoms and dark green leaves. Cherries prefer temperate climates, full sun, and well-drained soil.

Strawberry

Strawberry plants produce small, sweet, red fruits that are enjoyed worldwide. They have low-growing foliage and produce white flowers in spring. Strawberries thrive in full sun and well-drained, fertile soil.

Guava

Guava trees bear aromatic fruits that are rich in vitamins C and A. They have broad, oval leaves and white flowers. Guavas prefer tropical climates, full sun, and well-drained soil.

Grapes

Grapevines produce clusters of sweet or tart fruits that are used for eating fresh, making wine, and more. They have broad, lobed leaves and tendrils that allow them to climb. Grapes thrive in sunny locations with well-drained soil.

Apple

Apple trees are famous for their crisp, sweet fruits available in many varieties. They have beautiful spring blossoms and green, glossy leaves. Apples prefer temperate climates, full sun, and well-drained soil.
