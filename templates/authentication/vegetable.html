Grow Fresh vegetables

Tomato

Tomatoes are juicy, nutrient-rich fruits often used as vegetables in culinary dishes. They come in various colors, from vibrant reds to yellow and green, and are known for their tangy flavor and versatility in cooking.

Okra

Okra, also known as lady's fingers, is a flowering plant prized for its edible green seed pods. Commonly used in cuisines around the world, okra is valued for its distinct flavor, slimy texture (when cooked), and nutritional benefits.

Eggplant

Eggplants, also called aubergines, are glossy purple fruits commonly used as vegetables in cooking. They have a mild, slightly bitter taste and a spongy texture, making them ideal for dishes like moussaka, ratatouille, and eggplant parmesan.

Beans

Beans are nutritious legumes available in a variety of types, including green beans, kidney beans, and black beans. They are rich in protein, fiber, and essential nutrients, making them a staple in vegetarian diets and a versatile ingredient in soups, salads, and main dishes

Cucumber

Cucumbers are refreshing, crunchy vegetables with high water content, making them hydrating and nutritious. They are commonly eaten raw in salads, sandwiches, and pickles, and are prized for their cooling effect and mild, refreshing flavor.

Carrot

Carrots are vibrant orange root vegetables known for their sweet flavor and crunchy texture. Packed with beta-carotene, vitamins, and minerals, carrots are not only delicious raw or cooked but also offer numerous health benefits for vision, skin, and immune function.

Chilli

Chilies, or hot peppers, are spicy fruits widely used to add heat and flavor to dishes. Available in various shapes, sizes, and levels of spiciness, chilies are rich in capsaicin, which provides their characteristic heat and offers potential health benefits like pain relief and metabolism boost.

Pumkins

Pumpkins are large, orange winter squash prized for their sweet flesh and seeds. Used in both sweet and savory dishes, pumpkins are rich in vitamins, fiber, and antioxidants. They are popularly used in pies, soups, and roasted dishes during the fall season.
